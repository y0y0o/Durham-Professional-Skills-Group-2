## Overview

This poster applies innovation frameworks to the social challenge of AI ethics, exploring whether rapid AI development poses risks to human autonomy, psychological well-being, and social equity.

---

## Poster Structure

### 1. Motivation & Key Question
The poster opens with a central provocation — *"When algorithms know you better than you know yourself, how much free will remains?"* — and motivates the topic by highlighting that AI progress increasingly outpaces ethical regulation.

### 2. Stakeholder Analysis — Users
Using a structured issue-analysis framework, the poster identifies three core gaps in current AI governance:
- Risk analysis remains too conceptual, lacking differentiation between vulnerable user groups (e.g. teenagers vs. adults, users with depression)
- Existing reports lack mechanism-level explanation of *how* AI systems create psychological dependency
- Governance measures are too general, missing concrete and actionable intervention strategies

### 3. Deep Dive: Who Becomes "High-Risk"
This section examines how opaque AI scoring systems disproportionately affect low-income applicants, migrants, ethnic minorities, and disabled users — and how a single "high-risk" label can result in exclusion from housing, employment, or public services.

### 4. Building a Human-Centred AI Future
Five actionable recommendations are proposed:
1. **Mandate Explainability** — pre-deployment ethical impact assessments
2. **Restore Trust** — appeal mechanisms and algorithmic audit committees
3. **Embed Ethics in Education** — empathy-driven developer and policy training
4. **Human-Centred Design** — user participation and journey mapping
5. **Comprehensive Legislation** — drawing on frameworks such as the EU AI Act
